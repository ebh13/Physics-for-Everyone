Why This Matters

In 2023, the UNESCO Institute for Statistics reported that over 258 million children, adolescents, and youth were out of school globally, many of them lacking access to quality science education. Even among enrolled students, physics is often seen as one of the least accessible subjects due to the cost of materials, limited school resources, and a lack of hands-on, intuitive learning tools.

Physics is not just an academic subject ‚Äî it is a fundamental way of understanding the universe we live in. From energy to medicine, from space exploration to environmental technologies, physics drives innovation. Yet, many brilliant minds never get the chance to discover their potential because access to this knowledge is unevenly distributed.

By providing free, interactive educational tools, we hope to empower students, hobbyists, and self-learners who might otherwise be left out. Our goal is to break the barriers between passion and opportunity, and create a space where learning is truly open to everyone.
