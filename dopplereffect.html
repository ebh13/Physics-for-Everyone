The Doppler Effect: A Journey into Motion and Sound

Imagine This...

You're standing by a quiet street, and in the distance, you hear an ambulance rushing toward you. As it gets closer, the siren becomes higher, sharper, almost like it's screaming at you. The moment it flies past, the siren instantly drops into a deeper howl, fading away into the distance. You didn't move. The ambulance didn't change its siren. Yet, somehow, what you heard changed dramatically.

How Does It Work?

When a sound source moves toward you, the waves it produces bunch up. This makes the frequency higher â€” meaning you hear a higher-pitched sound.

When it moves away from you, the waves spread out. This makes the frequency lower â€” meaning a deeper, lower sound.

The same thing happens with light from stars. When stars move away from us, their light shifts towards red (longer waves). When they move towards us, it shifts towards blue (shorter waves). This "cosmic Doppler Effect" is how we know the universe is expanding!
